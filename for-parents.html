For Families

What Parents Want to Know

Everything you need to feel confident about bringing your child to Little Roots — from what a class actually looks like to how to get started.

Inside a Class

What a Little Roots Class Looks Like

No recitals. No pressure. Just movement, music, and a lot of joyful expression.

Warm-Up Circle

Classes open with a welcoming movement circle — gentle stretches, name games, and breathing exercises that help children arrive in their bodies and feel safe in the space.

Guided Movement & Music

Miss Ashley guides children through themed movement sequences — think "move like a rainstorm" or "show me what brave looks like." Music anchors each activity and keeps energy flowing.

Props & Play

Scarves, ribbons, shakers, stretch bands — all brought and managed by Miss Ashley. Props are a way in for children who are shy about "dancing" but happy to wave a scarf.

Free Invention Time

Near the end of each session, children get unstructured time to move however they want. This is where the magic often happens — wild spins, floor rolls, and solo performances for no one but themselves.

Getting Ready

What to Bring

Comfy Clothes

Anything your child can move freely in. Stretchy pants, leggings, a t-shirt. No special dancewear needed — ever.

A Water Bottle

Moving is thirsty work. Label it with your child's name and they're set.

Big Imaginations

That's it. Everything else — the music, the props, the curriculum — arrives with Miss Ashley.

Common Questions

Parent FAQ

My child is shy. Will they be okay?

Absolutely. Some children jump in immediately; others take a session or two to warm up. Miss Ashley never forces participation — she creates space, makes it irresistible, and lets each child come in at their own pace. Shy kids often end up the most expressive once they feel safe.

Can I stay and watch?

Yes — caregivers are welcome to observe at community events like Wiggle and Move. For school-based programs, parents aren't typically in the room, but you're welcome to ask the school about their observation policy.

What if my child has a developmental difference or disability?

Little Roots classes are adaptive by design. Miss Ashley has worked extensively with children across a wide range of abilities — including children with developmental disabilities, sensory processing differences, and varying attention spans. If your child has specific needs, reach out before the first class so Miss Ashley can be prepared to welcome them well.

What age is the right age to start?

Classes are designed for children ages 2–6. Two-year-olds are welcome with a caregiver alongside them. The sweet spot is often 3–5, when children have enough body awareness to engage with guided movement but are still fully in the magical "anything goes" stage of play.

Does my child need any dance experience?

None whatsoever. Little Roots is not a dance training program — it's creative movement. Children who have never set foot in a studio and children who have been in classes for years both find something here. The goal is expression, not technique.
