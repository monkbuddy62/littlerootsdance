Our Community Approach

Movement Is for Everyone

Our passion is to connect the body and mind through imaginative play and storytelling, song, dance, and sensory development. Community events are where that mission lives most freely — no pressure, no performance, just children moving and discovering what their bodies can do.

We teach a variety of mixed curriculum that adapts to the individuals and community it surrounds. Every session feels a little different because every group of children is a little different.

From the Blog

The Joy of Movement in Early Childhood

By Ashley Thompson  ·  October 25, 2025

When a two-year-old discovers that she can spin in a circle until she falls down laughing, something important is happening. She is learning about her vestibular system, yes — but she is also discovering that her body is hers to experiment with, that movement can be delightful, and that the floor is a perfectly acceptable place to end up.

Early childhood is a window of profound physical and neurological development. Children who are given regular opportunities for free and guided movement build stronger neural pathways, develop richer emotional vocabularies, and arrive in kindergarten with bodies that are ready to sit still — because they have had every chance to not sit still first.

Creative movement, in particular, asks children to translate inner experience into physical action. When I say "move like you're made of honey" or "show me what sad looks like with just your arms," I am asking them to do something genuinely complex: to feel, to imagine, and to express — all at once. The children who find this hardest are often the ones who need it most.

This is why I built Little Roots. Not to train dancers, but to give children a language that lives in their bodies — one that will serve them long after the music stops.

— Miss Ashley
