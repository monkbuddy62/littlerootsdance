The Instructor

Meet Miss Ashley

West Seattle native. Children's advocate. Creative movement teacher. The person who shows up to your school with a bag full of scarves and leaves every child feeling like they can fly.

Ashley Thompson

A Life Spent With Children

Ashley Thompson grew up in West Seattle and never really left — not because she couldn't, but because this corner of the city raised her, and she wanted to give something back to it. From the time she was old enough to notice that younger children needed looking after, she was looking after them.

That instinct eventually became a career — and then a calling. Before Little Roots existed, Ashley was coordinating emergency childcare through the Red Cross during disaster relief efforts, providing stability and care to children in some of the most disorienting moments of their young lives. She was also teaching adaptive swim lessons to children with developmental disabilities, learning how to communicate movement and safety across a wide spectrum of ability and experience.

Those experiences shaped a teacher who is patient, adaptable, and genuinely invested in every child in the room — not just the ones who take to it easily.

For over ten years, Ashley has been teaching creative movement and dance across Seattle and surrounding areas, building a practice grounded in positive reinforcement, imaginative play, and the belief that every child has a unique movement vocabulary worth discovering. Little Roots is the fullest expression of that work.

When she's not teaching, you'll find her somewhere in West Seattle — probably near the water, probably moving.

Background & Experience

More Than a Dance Teacher

Ashley brings a depth of child-centered experience that shapes every class she teaches.

Emergency Childcare Coordinator

Red Cross certified coordinator of emergency childcare during active disaster relief operations — bringing calm and care to children in crisis.

Adaptive Swim Instructor

Taught adaptive swim lessons for children with developmental disabilities, developing deep expertise in meeting each learner exactly where they are.

10+ Years of Creative Movement

Over a decade of teaching dance and creative movement across Seattle and surrounding areas, in schools, community centers, and private settings.

Native West Seattlite

Rooted in this community for her entire life. Ashley teaches here because this is home — and she knows these families, these schools, and these children.

Inclusive & Adaptive Teaching

Every class is adapted to the developmental range of the children present. Miss Ashley does not have a one-size curriculum — she has a curriculum that fits.

Positive Reinforcement Approach

Ashley's classroom philosophy is built on encouragement, not correction. Children learn best when they feel safe to try — and that is the environment she creates.

Teaching Philosophy

"Our passion is to connect the body and mind through imaginative play and storytelling, song, dance, and sensory development. We teach a variety of mixed curriculum that adapts to the individuals and community it surrounds. Creativity through expression — and positive reinforcement builds the confidence to keep expressing."
— Ashley Thompson, Miss Ashley, LLC
