The Science of Play

Why Movement Matters in Early Childhood

Creative movement is so much more than dance. It's how young children process the world, connect with others, and build the foundations for lifelong wellbeing.

Physical

Builds gross motor skills, coordination, balance, and spatial awareness through joyful, purposeful movement.

Cognitive

Strengthens listening, sequencing, and problem-solving as children follow cues, create patterns, and invent stories.

Emotional

Supports emotional regulation and self-expression. Positive reinforcement in class builds real, lasting confidence.

Social

Group movement teaches turn-taking, collaboration, and empathy — skills that carry well beyond the dance circle.

Meet Your Instructor

Miss Ashley Thompson

A native West Seattlite, Miss Ashley has spent her life working with and advocating for children. From coordinating emergency childcare during disaster relief to teaching adaptive swim lessons for children with developmental disabilities, her background is as rich as it is compassionate.

For over ten years she has brought creative movement across Seattle and surrounding areas, cultivating a classroom built on positive reinforcement, joyful expression, and the deep belief that every child has something beautiful to say through their body.
