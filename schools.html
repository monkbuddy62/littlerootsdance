What Students Gain

Movement Goals for Every Child

Each session is built around concrete developmental outcomes — not just fun, though there's plenty of that too.

Spatial Awareness

Children learn to navigate space, understand personal boundaries, and relate their body to others safely.

Coordination

Guided movement sequences build bilateral coordination, balance, and fine and gross motor skills simultaneously.

Emotional Regulation

Movement is a powerful tool for processing emotion. Sessions include calming sequences and expressive free-form time.

Group Collaboration

Partner and group movement activities reinforce the social-emotional skills children are building throughout their school day.

Designed for Your Team

Supporting Classroom Teachers

Your staff stay present. They don't have to prep a thing.

We ask that classroom teachers remain in the room during sessions — not to lead, but to provide gentle redirection when needed. Young children thrive best when familiar adults help set the tone, especially little ones still learning their "listening ears." Miss Ashley handles all instruction, transitions, and energy management.

Sometimes a child may need a quiet moment to reset before rejoining. We encourage teachers to offer an alternate calming activity nearby — like reading a book — so the child can regulate and return to the group when they're ready. This keeps the environment supportive and inclusive for everyone in the room.

Teachers often find these sessions a chance to see their students in a completely new context — and occasionally, when the wiggles are especially contagious, to join right in.

Every school is a little different. Miss Ashley is happy to talk through how this works for your specific space and group.
